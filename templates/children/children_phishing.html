What is Phishing?

Hello little explorer! When you're having fun online — playing games, watching videos, or chatting with friends — some sneaky tricksters might be hiding out there too! 👀

They try to "phish" for your secrets — like passwords, your address, or your parent's credit card! But don't worry — you can outsmart them easily!

1. Don't Click on Strange Links

Sometimes messages say:

  • 🎁 "You won a free iPad! Click here!"
  • ⚠️ "Your game account will be deleted! Hurry and log in here!"

These are fake! Always check with a grown-up before clicking anything weird or urgent.

2. Keep Your Secrets Super Safe

  • 🕵️ Never share your password — even if they say they're from the game!
  • 🏠 Don't tell your name, address, or school online.
  • 🐉 Use silly but strong passwords like "FluffyBananaDragon!" and keep them secret!

3. Look Carefully — Spot the Clues!

Phishing messages often:

  • ❌ Have bad spelling
  • 🤨 Say strange or silly things
  • 🌐 Use weird web addresses like "www.realname.com"

If it looks strange, it probably is! Be a detective and find the clues!

4. Real Friends Don't Ask for Secrets

  • 💬 "Tell me your password, I'll give you free coins!"
  • 📩 "I'm your online buddy! Where do you live?"

🚫 Say: "No, thank you!" A real friend won't ask those things. Always tell a trusted adult if something feels off.

5. What Should You Do If You See Something Strange?

  • 🙅‍♀️ Don't reply.
  • 🖱️ Don't click anything.
  • 👨‍👩‍👧 Show it to a parent, teacher, or trusted adult.
  • 🛡️ Block the sender or get help to do it.

You're never alone — there's always someone who can help!

🎉 You're the Hero of Your Internet Adventure!

Every time you:

  • 🛑 Stop and think before clicking,
  • 🔐 Keep your secrets safe,
  • 🆘 Ask for help when unsure...

…you're being a brave and awesome Cyber Safety Hero! 🏅
